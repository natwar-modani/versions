a hot spot is a region of high or special activity within a larger area of low or normal activity; it may refer to:

 hotspot (geology), a location above a hot upwelling plume of mantle.
 hot spot, a keno game offered by the california lottery
 hot spot (veterinary medicine)
 at an airport, places where collisions between aircraft and ground equipment may occur, are referred as hot spots

computer science
 a region of computer memory where the program counter is frequently found during program execution, possibly indicating code in need of optimization. see performance analysis.
 software framework hot spots describe the variation points in software frameworks. 
 hotspot (wi-fi), a wi-fi access point or area, in particular for connecting to the internet
 hotspot, a java virtual machine from sun microsystems
 screen hotspots, especially in  gui applications, provide areas on which computer-users click or hover for extra information-processing

biology

 recombination hotspot, dna positions where genetic recombination occurs with above-average frequency
