 

izabella scorupco (or skorupko) (born june 4, 1970) is an actress who is most famous for appearing as bond girl natalya simonova in the 1995 james bond film goldeneye.

 biography 

born as izabela dorota skorupko in białystok, poland to lech and magdalena skorupko, she was only one year old when her parents separated and she came to live with her mother. in 1978, they moved to bredäng in stockholm, sweden. coming to sweden as an 8 year old, she learned to speak swedish, english and french.

up until 1989, she worked as model, travelling around europe, where she picked up a brief knowledge of the italian language, and appeared on the cover of vogue.

in 1987 she was discovered by director staffan hildebrand and starred in the film ingen kan älska som vi ("nobody can love like us"). it was said that she earned the role by pursuing the director and convincing him that she was right for the role. in the early nineties, she also had a brief but successful career as a pop singer, releasing the album iza which went gold in sweden.

on 25th december 1996, she married polish ice hockey player mariusz czerkawski, then a player for the edmonton oilers. they separated in 1998, and have one daughter together, julia (born september 15, 1997). on january 30, 2003 she married an american named jeffrey raymond; they have a son, jakob (born july 24, 2003) and live in los angeles.

in 2011, izabella reprised her singing career, duetting with peter jöback in his new single jag har dig nu, featuring in the music video and in his short extension film la vie, l'amour, le mort.

it has been confirmed that she will be hosting the new series of sweden's next top model in spring 2012.

her biggest fan is famously known as amy elouise.

 modelling 

scorupco has modelled for many high street brands, including tiffany and co. and h&m. she has also represented oriflame, dr irena eris and wera. she has worked with many photographers, normally based around los angeles, and many stylists, including jonas hallberg, appearing on his tv programme several times.
