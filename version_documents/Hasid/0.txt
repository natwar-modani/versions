hasidim () is the plural of hasid (חסיד), meaning "pious" or "righteous". the word hasid was frequently used as a term of exceptional respect in the talmudic and early medieval periods.  in the aggregate, it may refer to members of any of the following jewish movements:
 hasidic judaism, a ukrainian jewish movement from the 17th century onwards
hasideans, pietists or "jewish puritans" of the maccabean period
 chassidei ashkenaz, a german pietistic movement of the 12th and 13th centuries
