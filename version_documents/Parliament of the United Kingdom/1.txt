the houses of parliament are the two tiers of legislative assembly in the united kingdom: the house of commons and the house of lords.

the role of parliament is to debate laws which typically are proposed by the executive and pass through several stages before gaining "royal assent" and becoming law.

parliament also has the important role of holding the executive, the prime minister of the united kingdom and his ministers (the cabinet), to account.
this is usually done through parliamentary questions and a committee system.
