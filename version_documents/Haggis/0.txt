haggis is a traditional scottish dish made with the following ingredients: sheep's heart, liver and lungs, minced with onion, oatmeal, suet, spices, and salt, mixed with stock and traditionally boiled in the animal's stomach for several hours. it is usually served with turnips ("neeps") and potatoes.

nowadays haggis is traditionally served with the burns' supper on january 25th, when scotland's national poet, robert burns, is commemorated. (he wrote a poem about the haggis). in burns' day haggis was popular with poor farmers.
